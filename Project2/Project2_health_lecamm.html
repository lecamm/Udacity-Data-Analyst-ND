Introduction¶

This analysis uses the data available in GapMinder dataset on women health. The analysis looks more specifically into the different types of cancer causing death of women (breast, colon and rectum, liver, lung and stomach) and the worlwide evolution of the number of deaths caused by each one in the last decades. The evolution of some potential risk factors in the last decades is also analysed.

The following questions are targeted: how did the number of deaths of women caused by cancer evolve in the last decades? At the world level and for some specific countries?

#loading csv files from GapMinder dataset on health data (different cancer types and risks) into pandas dataframes   
files=listdir('Health')
temp=pd.read_csv('Health/'+files[0])
years=temp.columns[1:].astype('int64').to_list()
countries=temp.country.to_list()
df=pd.DataFrame([list(yc) for yc in product(years,countries)],columns=['years','countries'])
df[files[0][:-4]]=np.nan
for yc in product(years,countries):
    df.loc[(df.years==yc[0])&(df.countries==yc[1]),files[0][:-4]]=temp.loc[temp.country==yc[1],str(yc[0])].item()
#merge dataframes into one
for file in files[1:]:
    temp=pd.read_csv('Health/'+file)
    years=temp.columns[1:].astype('int64').to_list()
    countries=temp.country.to_list()
    temp_res=pd.DataFrame([list(yc) for yc in product(years,countries)],columns=['years', 'countries'])
    temp_res[file[:-4]]=np.nan
    for yc in product(years,countries):
        temp_res.loc[(temp_res.years==yc[0])&(temp_res.countries==yc[1]),file[:-4]]=temp.loc[temp.country==yc[1],str(yc[0])].item()
    df=df.merge(temp_res,on=['years','countries'],how='outer')

The analysis on the potential risk factors is not very conclusive. Two interesting risk factors (alcohol consumption and smoking) were discarded from the analysis because of the significant amount of data missing. The remaining risk factors do not present a significant variation on the available time period (1980 to 2008) and cannot really be used as explanatory variables for the types of cancer studied. Also the last 8 years of data are missing where an increase of deaths by cancer was observed.

Conclusions¶

A significant increase (34 to 48%) of number of women deaths caused by the breast, colon and rectum, liver and lung cancers has been observed at the world level from 1990 to 2016. The number of deaths caused by stomach cancer remained slightly constant at the world level in the last decades.

Most of the countries selected in the analysis (United States, Canada,'Brazil', 'India','China','Russia', 'France','Italy','Spain','Germany','United Kingdom', 'Algeria','Egypt','Nigeria','Ethiopia') presented an increase rate of women deaths caused by cancer higher than 20% from 1991 to 2016; except for United Kingdom (0.3%), Germany (4.7%) and Russia (8%). All selected countries presented a continuous increase of the number of deaths in the last five years.

The analysis on potential risk factors was not conclusive and should be discarded due to missing data.
